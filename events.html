13 times as many people identify as Irish abroad as there are in Ireland.

The vast majority of Irish emigrants today are young, well-educated and skilled people coming from an affluent country. They leave seeking opportunity, adventure or education abroad. With internet and travel accessibility now it's relative easy for emigrants to keep in touch with loved ones and to take trips home.


However, this was not always the case as our ancestors did not have such privilages coming from extremely impoverished circumstances. Many went from poverty in Ireland to poverty in a new land. Even worse still, many were exiled for their religion or political beliefs and forced into a life of servitude or slavery. Less than a century ago, an Irish person leaving their native country would be assumed to never set foot on it again.


Today the Irish are broadly welcomed around the world, but often Irish communities abroad were treated with disdain and discrimination. Therefore, it's important to consider the circumstances which led to the making of a some of our featured forgotten friends.



With the most remote Irish pubs not being in Donegal, the Kerry mountains or Aran Islands, but in the Himalayas, Patagonia, Mongolia and the Andes... it's clear to see how far Irish feet have travelled.

However, there are a number of countries in which the Irish mainly settled, either by choice, necessity or by force. The principle locations and events that caused the migrations were as follows:

Cause Period Destination
Cromwellian Conquest: Indentured Servitude & Slavery 1649-1653 The Carribbean, North America
Penal Colonies 1788-1868 Australia
Napoleonic Wars 1803-1815 Europe
South American Revolutions 1810-1829 Argentina, Uruguay, Chile, Brazil
The Great Famine 1845-1852 Britain, USA, Canada
The Boer Wars, Mining Opportunities 1880-1902 South Africa
Irish Economy 1950-1970, 1980-1990 Britain, North America
Global Recession 2008-2012 Britain, Australia, New Zealand, North America


Today


Over the last decade there has been a steady flow of Irish emigrants all across the globe. However in recent times, the reasons for leaving have changed from economic necessity to include better and more varied opportunities abroad, the cost of living and housing crisis in Ireland, chance to acrue wealth, adventure and a retirement in the sun. In addition to the above countries, today many Irish people are deciding to settle in the Middle-East, South-East Asia and the Mediterranean.
