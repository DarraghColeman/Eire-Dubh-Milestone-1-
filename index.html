About



Forget Saint Brendan and Joe Biden.

Never mind Maureen O'Hara and Barak O'Bama.

Éire Dubh looks at obscure figures and events of the Irish diaspora that maybe weren't much to write home about.


As we tend to laud the many successful and famous emigrants, and often attempting to lay claim on those with tenuous connections to our tiny island, many notable people and events abroad have gone under the radar. The phrase "Ah sure, we export all our best stuff!" isn't so true as not all those who wandered abroad brought craic agus ceol.


So for better or for worse, we bring you a collection of histories from the down-trodden and dark to down-right dispicable diaspora of Ireland.

The Diaspora

A small island with a big footprint.


From Tipperary to Timbuktu, the Irish are all over. So if asked at your next pub-quiz who the three astronauts on the first moon landing were, know that it was Neil Armstrong, Buzz Aldrin and...

...Michael Collins? No, not the Michael Collins posthumously sent on a diplomatic mission by an aging Eamon de Valera, but we'll claim him nonetheless!
